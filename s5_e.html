Phase 5: The Three Great Campaigns

September 1948 - January 1949 — The decisive military campaigns that destroyed Nationalist military power and guaranteed Communist victory.

Overview: The Turning Point

The Decisive Phase (September 1948 - January 1949)

In late 1948, the PLA launched three massive coordinated campaigns that shattered Nationalist military power and sealed the fate of the Chinese Civil War. The Liaoshen, Huaihai, and Pingjin campaigns involved hundreds of thousands of troops and resulted in the capture of China's major cities and the destruction of the KMT's best-trained forces. These victories made Communist control of mainland China inevitable.

  • Liaoshen Campaign (Sept-Nov 1948): The PLA defeated and destroyed the KMT's elite Manchurian forces, securing control of China's industrial northeast.
  • Huaihai Campaign (Nov 1948-Jan 1949): A massive 600,000-strong engagement in central China where the PLA annihilated the KMT's second-largest army group.
  • Pingjin Campaign (Nov 1948-Jan 1949): The PLA encircled and forced the surrender of Nationalist forces around Beijing and Tianjin.

The Three Campaigns in Detail

Liaoshen Campaign (Sept 12 - Nov 2, 1948)

Lin Biao's PLA forces launched an assault on Nationalist-held Manchuria, one of the most strategically valuable regions in China. The campaign saw the destruction of the KMT's 65th and 60th Armies.

  • Initial Phase: PLA besieged Changchun and Shenyang, cutting off supply lines and forcing Nationalist surrender or retreat.
  • Outcome: The Nationalists lost over 330,000 troops killed, wounded, or captured. Manchuria fell entirely to Communist control.
  • Significance: Manchuria's industrial capacity and natural resources now supported the PLA's war effort.

Huaihai Campaign (Nov 6, 1948 - Jan 10, 1949)

The largest and most strategically important of the three campaigns, fought in the plain between the Huai and Hai rivers in central China. Over 600,000 troops were engaged.

  • The Armies: Nationalist forces under Du Yuming and Huang Wei fought against Chen Yi and Su Yu's PLA armies.
  • The Battle: Despite initial Nationalist advantages, the PLA's superior mobility, coordination, and peasant logistics support proved decisive.
  • The Loss: Over 550,000 KMT troops killed, wounded, or captured — effectively the destruction of the Nationalist army's core strength.
  • Impact: Cleared the way for the PLA to advance on the Yangtze and capture southern cities.

Pingjin Campaign (Nov 29, 1948 - Jan 31, 1949)

The final campaign of this decisive phase involved the encirclement of Beijing (then called Peiping) and Tianjin, isolating massive Nationalist garrisons.

  • Siege of Beijing: The PLA surrounded the city but offered peaceful surrender terms, leading to the KMT abandonment of the capital.
  • Tianjin Falls: After the fall of Beijing, Tianjin's Nationalist garrison surrendered without major fighting.
  • Casualties: Over 500,000 Nationalist troops killed, wounded, or surrendered.
  • Outcome: Northern China was now firmly under Communist control; the Nationalists held only southern regions.

Why the Nationalists Failed

Strategic and Tactical Collapse

  • Overextension: KMT forces were spread across vast territories with weak supply lines, easy targets for the concentrated PLA.
  • Loss of Air Superiority: Without sufficient fuel and spare parts, Nationalist aircraft were grounded, eliminating their main technological advantage.
  • Defections: Thousands of Nationalist soldiers deserted or switched sides, swelling PLA ranks.
  • Collapse of Morale: Hyperinflation, military defeats, and peasant hostility destroyed soldier and civilian confidence in the Nationalist cause.
  • Poor Generalship: Nationalist commanders made tactical errors and failed to coordinate defense effectively.

Aftermath and Significance

The Path to Communist Victory

By January 1949, the three campaigns had destroyed or scattered the KMT's best armies. Chiang Kai-shek resigned as president on January 21, 1949 (though he would retain control). The Nationalists retreated to southern China, but their military defeat was terminal. The next phase—the PLA's crossing of the Yangtze in spring 1949—would complete the Communist conquest of mainland China.
