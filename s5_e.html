Phase 5: The Three Great Campaigns

September 1948 - January 1949 — The huge military battles that destroyed Nationalist military power and guaranteed the Communists would win.

Overview: The Turning Point

The Deciding Phase (September 1948 - January 1949)

In late 1948, the Communist army launched three huge coordinated battles that smashed Nationalist military power and decided the fate of the Chinese Civil War. The Liaoshen, Huaihai, and Pingjin campaigns involved hundreds of thousands of troops and resulted in capturing China's major cities and destroying the Nationalists' best-trained forces. These wins made Communist control of mainland China inevitable.

  • Liaoshen Campaign (Sept-Nov 1948): The Communist army defeated and destroyed the Nationalists' best Manchurian forces, taking control of China's industrial northeast.
  • Huaihai Campaign (Nov 1948-Jan 1949): A massive battle with 600,000 troops in central China where the Communist army wiped out the Nationalists' second-largest army group.
  • Pingjin Campaign (Nov 1948-Jan 1949): The Communist army surrounded and forced Nationalist forces around Beijing and Tianjin to surrender.

The Three Campaigns in Detail

Liaoshen Campaign (Sept 12 - Nov 2, 1948)

Lin Biao's Communist forces launched an attack on Nationalist-held Manchuria, one of the most important regions in China. The campaign destroyed the Nationalists' 65th and 60th Armies.

  • First Phase: The Communist army surrounded Changchun and Shenyang, cutting off supply lines and forcing Nationalist surrender or retreat.
  • Result: The Nationalists lost over 330,000 troops killed, wounded, or captured. Manchuria fell completely to Communist control.
  • Why It Mattered: Manchuria's factories and natural resources now supported the Communist army's war effort.

Huaihai Campaign (Nov 6, 1948 - Jan 10, 1949)

The biggest and most important of the three campaigns, fought in the plains between the Huai and Hai rivers in central China. Over 600,000 troops fought.

  • The Armies: Nationalist forces under Du Yuming and Huang Wei fought against Chen Yi and Su Yu's Communist armies.
  • The Battle: Despite having advantages at first, the Communist army's better movement, teamwork, and supply support from farmers proved decisive.
  • The Loss: Over 550,000 Nationalist troops killed, wounded, or captured — basically destroying the Nationalist army's main strength.
  • Impact: Cleared the way for the Communist army to advance to the Yangtze River and capture southern cities.

Pingjin Campaign (Nov 29, 1948 - Jan 31, 1949)

The final campaign of this deciding phase involved surrounding Beijing (then called Peiping) and Tianjin, trapping huge Nationalist garrisons.

  • Siege of Beijing: The Communist army surrounded the city but offered peaceful surrender terms, leading to the Nationalists abandoning the capital.
  • Tianjin Falls: After Beijing fell, Tianjin's Nationalist garrison gave up without major fighting.
  • Casualties: Over 500,000 Nationalist troops killed, wounded, or surrendered.
  • Result: Northern China was now firmly under Communist control; the Nationalists only held southern regions.

Why the Nationalists Failed

Strategic and Tactical Collapse

  • Overextension: KMT forces were spread across vast territories with weak supply lines, easy targets for the concentrated PLA.
  • Loss of Air Superiority: Without sufficient fuel and spare parts, Nationalist aircraft were grounded, eliminating their main technological advantage.
  • Defections: Thousands of Nationalist soldiers deserted or switched sides, swelling PLA ranks.
  • Collapse of Morale: Hyperinflation, military defeats, and peasant hostility destroyed soldier and civilian confidence in the Nationalist cause.
  • Poor Generalship: Nationalist commanders made tactical errors and failed to coordinate defense effectively.

Aftermath and Significance

The Path to Communist Victory

By January 1949, the three campaigns had destroyed or scattered the KMT's best armies. Chiang Kai-shek resigned as president on January 21, 1949 (though he would retain control). The Nationalists retreated to southern China, but their military defeat was terminal. The next phase—the PLA's crossing of the Yangtze in spring 1949—would complete the Communist conquest of mainland China.
