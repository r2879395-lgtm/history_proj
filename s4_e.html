Overview: Building Durable Power

Taking Control and Governing

During early 1948, the Chinese Communists focused on turning military wins into stable political control. They set up local governments, expanded land reform that gave land to farmers, and created grassroots groups that connected the Party to village life. These moves strengthened support from regular people and provided the political and supply base for later big attacks.

  • Land Reform: Taking land from rich landlords and giving it to farmers strengthened farmer loyalty and destroyed Nationalist power in the countryside.
  • Local Government: Farming cooperatives, tax changes, and Communist officials settling disputes created working governments in freed areas.
  • Security & Supplies: The Communist army secured countryside supply lines and organized militia groups that protected villages and supported frontline fighting.

Regional Consolidation

Northern & Central Plains

The Communists took control across big chunks of the north and central plains by clearing out isolated Nationalist garrisons and connecting freed countryside to city supply hubs. Control over farming provided recruits, food, and taxes that strengthened Communist fighting ability.

  • Shandong & Hebei: Military campaigns and political work isolated Nationalist garrisons, letting the Communist army control important transportation and farming regions.
  • Manchuria (legacy): Earlier Soviet withdrawal left behind captured Japanese equipment and factories that the Communists used for later attacks.

Effects on the Nationalists

Why the Nationalists Couldn't Take Back Control

  • Hit-and-Run Pressure: The Communist army's mix of getting people involved politically and military pressure made large-scale Nationalist takeovers too expensive and impossible to keep up.
  • Running Out of Resources: The Nationalists' economic problems, including crazy inflation and supply breakdowns, destroyed popular support and fighting ability.
  • Losing Countryside Support: Land reform and local government programs made farmers less willing to help Nationalist forces.

Strategic Importance

Setting Up for the 1948 Battles

Taking firm control in early 1948 let the Communists turn local advantages into the ability to move armies around. By getting supplies, soldiers, and political control in the countryside, the Communists set things up for the huge battles later in 1948 that would kick the Nationalists out of most of mainland China.
