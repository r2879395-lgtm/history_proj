Phase 6: The Yangtze Crossing

April - May 1949 — The PLA breaches the Yangtze River, historically the dividing line between north and south China, and captures the Nationalist capital.

Overview: Breaking the Last Barrier

Crossing the Yangtze

In April 1949, the PLA crossed the Yangtze River, the traditional dividing line between north and south China. Once this last natural defensive barrier was breached, Nationalist control of the mainland collapsed quickly. Within weeks, Nanjing, the Nationalist capital, fell with little resistance, and Communist forces pushed on to seize Shanghai and other key cities along the lower Yangtze.

Why Resistance Crumbled

The Yangtze campaign showed how weak the Nationalists had become after earlier defeats. Their best units had already been destroyed in the north, leaving poorly supplied, demoralized troops to defend a huge river line. Many officers and officials had fled south or planned to escape overseas. Soldiers went unpaid as hyperinflation ruined the currency, and civilians were unwilling to sacrifice for a government they blamed for corruption and chaos.

Once the PLA established bridgeheads on the southern bank, organized Nationalist resistance in the lower Yangtze region collapsed. Cities like Nanjing and Shanghai fell after brief fighting or negotiated surrenders. By late spring, the Nationalist government was retreating toward the far south and to the island of Taiwan, while Communist leaders were already planning how to rule a unified mainland.

Revolution, Reaction, and Reform

The Yangtze crossing turned revolutionary warfare into full national takeover: the PLA now controlled China’s political and economic heartland. The Nationalists reacted by abandoning one base after another and concentrating their remaining troops and gold reserves on Taiwan, hoping to survive as a government in exile. In the newly captured cities, Communist authorities presented themselves as restorers of order—promising to stabilize prices, punish corruption, and begin rebuilding after years of war. This phase closed the military struggle on the mainland and opened the path to proclaiming the People’s Republic of China.
