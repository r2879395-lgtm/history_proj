Phase 7: The Final Outcome

June - December 1949 — The establishment of the People's Republic of China and the permanent retreat of Nationalist forces to Taiwan.

Overview: Victory and Division

The War’s Final Months

After the fall of Shanghai in May 1949, organized Nationalist resistance on the mainland quickly unraveled. PLA forces advanced through south and southwest China, occupying remaining strongholds with limited fighting as many Nationalist units surrendered or slipped away. At the same time, Chiang Kai-shek and his government began a massive retreat across the Taiwan Strait, moving troops, officials, and resources to the island that would become their long-term base.

A New State is Proclaimed

On October 1, 1949, Mao Zedong stood at Tiananmen in Beijing and announced the founding of the Peoples Republic of China, declaring that the Chinese people have stood up. This ceremony symbolized the end of decades of civil war and foreign intervention and the beginning of Communist rule over the mainland. While the PRC consolidated power on the continent, the Nationalist Republic of China survived on Taiwan under martial law, claiming to remain the legitimate government of all China.

The war’s outcome reshaped the Cold War. A vast new Communist state now bordered the Soviet Union and Korea, while the United States increasingly backed Taiwan. The division across the Taiwan Strait, created in 1949, has remained one of the most sensitive issues in global politics for more than seventy years.

Revolution, Reaction, and Reform

With the creation of the PRC, the Communist revolution completed its takeover of mainland China, replacing the old Republic with a socialist one-party state. The Nationalists reacted by entrenching themselves on Taiwan, building a heavily militarized, authoritarian regime that later transformed into a separate democracy. On the mainland, Communist leaders launched sweeping reforms—land redistribution, campaigns against “counterrevolutionaries,” and tighter state control over industry—to secure their new rule and remake Chinese society. The Chinese Civil War thus ended not with full unity, but with a lasting divide between two rival governments claiming to represent China.
