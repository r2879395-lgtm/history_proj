Phase 3: Shift in Momentum

The PLA's Strategic Defense and Counterattack (1947) — How the Communists turned the tide of the Chinese Civil War.

Overview: The Turning Point

1947: A Year of Strategic Transformation

By 1947, the Nationalist offensive had stalled. Despite capturing Yan'an in March 1947, the KMT's military advantage began to erode. The People's Liberation Army (PLA) adapted to conventional warfare while maintaining guerrilla tactics, launching devastating counterattacks that would ultimately reverse the course of the war.

  • Tactical Shift: The PLA transitioned from pure guerrilla warfare to large-scale mobile warfare, combining both strategies effectively.
  • Nationalist Overextension: KMT forces spread too thin across vast territories, unable to hold and supply all captured areas.
  • Lin Biao's Leadership in Manchuria: The legendary general orchestrated brilliant campaigns that gave the Communists control of China's industrial heartland.
  • Peasant Support: CCP land reform policies won massive rural support, providing soldiers, supplies, and intelligence networks.

Key Campaigns and Battles

Major Offensives of 1947

  • Shanxi-Chahar-Hebei Campaign (Jan-March 1947): The PLA repelled a major Nationalist drive, demonstrating improved coordination and firepower.
  • Capture of Yan'an (March 1947): While the Nationalists took the symbolic Communist capital, the PLA's main forces escaped intact, later recapturing it.
  • Manchurian Offensives (Summer-Fall 1947): Lin Biao's forces launched counterattacks in the industrial northeast, eventually establishing firm control over strategic cities like Harbin and Changchun.
  • Shandong Peninsula Campaign (1947): The PLA secured control of this vital region, cutting off Nationalist supply lines and isolating Nationalist forces in northern cities.

Strategic Factors Behind the Shift

Why the KMT Momentum Faltered

  • • Logistics Crisis: KMT supply lines stretched too far; garrison troops were isolated and vulnerable to PLA ambush and siege tactics.
  • • Inflation & Economic Collapse: Rampant Nationalist inflation destroyed soldier morale and made recruitment difficult.
  • • Corruption & Poor Generalship: Ineffective KMT leadership squandered numerical and material advantages.
  • • Soviet Support for the CCP: Soviet control of Manchuria until early 1946, combined with captured Japanese weapons, gave the PLA superior equipment.
  • • American Exhaustion: U.S. aid to the Nationalists declined as American public opinion shifted against further intervention in China.

The PLA's Military Innovation

How Mao & Lin Biao Revolutionized Communist Strategy

The PLA's success in 1947 came from blending Mao's theories of people's war with practical large-scale operations:

  • • Mobile Warfare: Large PLA units moved rapidly across vast distances, striking Nationalist positions unexpectedly.
  • • Mass Base Mobilization: Peasants provided recruitment, supplies, and intelligence, creating an unstoppable resource advantage.
  • • Concentration of Forces: The PLA learned to mass overwhelming force against isolated Nationalist garrisons, then disperse before relief arrived.
  • • Political Education: Communist cadres integrated with armies, maintaining discipline and ideological commitment.
