The Chinese Civil War

A National History Day Investigation: The 7 Pivotal Phases (1945–1949)

Explore the turning points that shaped modern China's political future

By: Ryan Kuo and Elliott Chu

1

Phase 1: Post-War Power Vacuum

The Race for Territory (August - December 1945)

After Japan gave up, both sides rushed to grab major cities and important places. The U.S. helped fly Nationalist troops around, while the Soviets gave weapons to the Communists in Manchuria.

View Phase Details →
2

Phase 2: The Nationalist Offensive

Initial KMT Push & CCP Retrenchment (1946)

Chiang Kai-shek launched a huge attack. The Nationalists took important Communist cities like Yan'an and had better weapons, but they spread their forces too thin and made bad choices about where to put their troops.

View Phase Details →
3

Phase 3: Shift in Momentum

The PLA’s Strategic Defense and Counterattack (1947)

The Communist army figured out how to use guerrilla tactics and started fighting back successfully. Lin Biao led attacks in Manchuria that turned the Nationalists' wins into problems for them.

View Phase Details →
4

Phase 4: Consolidation of Base Areas

Northern & Central Plains Dominance (Early 1948)

The Communists built up strong home bases and won support from farmers by giving them land. Meanwhile, the Nationalists struggled with supply problems and their soldiers' morale fell apart across northern and central China.

View Phase Details →
5

Phase 5: The Three Great Campaigns

The Strategic Blunder (September 1948 - January 1949)

Three huge battles—Liaoshen, Huaihai, and Pingjin—where the Nationalists lost their best troops and most important cities. After this, it was clear the Communists were going to win.

View Phase Details →
6

Phase 6: The Yangtze Crossing

The Collapse of the South (April - May 1949)

The Communist army crossed the Yangtze River, splitting the Nationalists in half. They took Nanjing (the Nationalist capital) and then captured big cities like Shanghai.

View Phase Details →
7

Phase 7: The Final Outcome

Establishment of the PRC and Retreat (June - December 1949)

The war ended with Mao Zedong announcing the creation of the People's Republic of China in Beijing. Chiang Kai-shek and his remaining forces escaped to Taiwan for good.
