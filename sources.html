Sources & Bibliography

A comprehensive collection of primary and secondary sources used in researching the Chinese Civil War (1945-1949).

PRIMARY SOURCES

Contemporary Documents & Firsthand Accounts

Original documents, memoirs, and firsthand testimonies from the period.

Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference. "The Common Program of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference." September 29, 1949. Common Program Science. Accessed December 8, 2025.

The Common Program (1949) was the provisional constitution of the nascent PRC. It defined China as a "People's Democratic" state under the leadership of the working class, abolished old imperialist structures, and established the political, economic, and social foundations of the new regime.

Alphahis and Jung Chang. "Jung Chang on Life in Nationalist China (1986)." Chinese Revolution. August 14, 2024. Accessed November 7, 2025. https://alphahistory.com/chineserevolution/jung-chang-economy-guomindang-1986/

Jung Chang's firsthand account of life in Nationalist China, describing extreme poverty, currency collapse, and economic hardship under Kuomintang rule that contributed to public support for revolution.

Alphahis. "Dean Acheson on the Outcome of the Chinese Revolution (1949)." Chinese Revolution. August 14, 2024. Accessed November 14, 2025. https://alphahistory.com/chineserevolution/dean-acheson-chinese-revolution-1949/

U.S. Secretary of State Dean Acheson's analysis explaining that the Kuomintang's fall was due to lack of soldier morale and declining government popularity rather than military defeats.

Mao Zedong. Selected Works of Mao Tse-tung, Vols. 4-5. Beijing: Foreign Languages Press, 1961-1977. Print.

United States Department of State. United States Relations with China: With Special Reference to the Period 1944-1949 (The China White Paper). Washington, D.C.: U.S. Government Printing Office, 1949. Print.

Marshall, George C. "The Marshall Mission to China, December 1945-January 1947." Foreign Relations of the United States, Vol. 7: The Far East: China. Washington, D.C.: U.S. Government Printing Office, 1971. Print.

Chiang Kai-shek. Soviet Russia in China: A Summing-Up at Seventy. New York: Farrar, Straus and Cudahy, 1957. Print.

SECONDARY SOURCES

Scholarly Books & Monographs

Academic works by leading historians and scholars of modern Chinese history.

Biscontini, Tyler J. "Chinese Communist Revolution (1949)." EBSCO Research Starters. EBSCO. Accessed November 3, 2025. https://www.ebsco.com/research-starters/history/chinese-communist-revolution-1949

Comprehensive overview of the power vacuum after WWII, the CCP's rise, and the consequences of Mao's policies including the Great Leap Forward.

Office of the Historian. "The Chinese Revolution of 1949." Milestones in the History of U.S. Foreign Relations. U.S. Department of State. October 14, 2014. Accessed November 3, 2025. https://history.state.gov/milestones/1945-1952/chinese-rev

Official U.S. State Department analysis of the 1949 Revolution, U.S. support for the KMT, and Taiwan's role in the UN until the 1970s.

Alphahis. "Complaints of Hunger From the Great Leap Forward (1959)." Alpha History. August 14, 2024. Accessed November 17, 2025. https://alphahistory.com/chineserevolution/complaints-hunger-great-leap-forward-1959/

Examines the aftermath of the Chinese Revolution and the failed Great Leap Forward (1958-1962), showing widespread famine and peasant revolts under CCP rule.

Westad, Odd Arne. Decisive Encounters: The Chinese Civil War, 1946-1950. Stanford: Stanford University Press, 2003. Print.

Pepper, Suzanne. Civil War in China: The Political Struggle, 1945-1949. 2nd ed. Lanham, MD: Rowman & Littlefield Publishers, 1999. Print.

Levine, Steven I. Anvil of Victory: The Communist Revolution in Manchuria, 1945-1948. New York: Columbia University Press, 1987. Print.

Lary, Diana. China's Civil War: A Social History, 1945-1949. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 2015. Print.

Tanner, Harold M. The Battle for Manchuria and the Fate of China: Siping, 1946. Bloomington: Indiana University Press, 2013. Print.

Eastman, Lloyd E. Seeds of Destruction: Nationalist China in War and Revolution, 1937-1949. Stanford: Stanford University Press, 1984. Print.

Academic Journal Publications

Peer-reviewed articles from leading academic journals.

Shen, Zhihua and Yafeng Xia. "Leadership Transfer in the Asian Revolution: Mao Zedong and the Asian Cominform." Cold War History 14.2 (2014): 195-213. Web.

Sheng, Michael M. "Mao, Stalin, and the Formation of the Anti-Japanese United Front: 1935-37." The China Quarterly 129 (1992): 149-170. Web.

Chassin, Lionel Max. "The Three Great Campaigns of 1948-1949." The Communist Conquest of China: A History of the Civil War 1945-1949. Cambridge: Harvard University Press, 1965. 166-201. Print.

DIGITAL RESOURCES

Online Archives & Multimedia

Digital collections, online historical resources, and educational videos.

Yan Xishan. "The Chinese Civil War (1945 and Beyond): Every Day." YouTube video, June 28, 2019. Accessed November 15, 2025.

Detailed day-by-day visualization of territorial control fluctuations during the Chinese Civil War (1945-1949), showing which side controlled which parts of China throughout the conflict.

Wilson Center Digital Archive. "Chinese Civil War Collection." History and Public Policy Program Digital Archive. Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars. Web. Accessed 2024.

Stanford University. "Chinese Civil War Resources." Hoover Institution Archives. Web. Accessed 2024.

Library of Congress. "Chinese Civil War Photograph Collection." Prints and Photographs Division. Web. Accessed 2024.
