Sources & Bibliography

A comprehensive collection of primary and secondary sources used in researching the Chinese Civil War (1945-1949). All sources are cited in proper academic format.

PRIMARY SOURCES

Contemporary Documents & Firsthand Accounts

Original documents, memoirs, and firsthand testimonies from the period.

Mao Zedong. Selected Works of Mao Tse-tung, Vols. 4-5. Beijing: Foreign Languages Press, 1961-1977. Print.

United States Department of State. United States Relations with China: With Special Reference to the Period 1944-1949 (The China White Paper). Washington, D.C.: U.S. Government Printing Office, 1949. Print.

Marshall, George C. "The Marshall Mission to China, December 1945-January 1947." Foreign Relations of the United States, Vol. 7: The Far East: China. Washington, D.C.: U.S. Government Printing Office, 1971. Print.

Chiang Kai-shek. Soviet Russia in China: A Summing-Up at Seventy. New York: Farrar, Straus and Cudahy, 1957. Print.

SECONDARY SOURCES

Scholarly Books & Monographs

Academic works by leading historians and scholars of modern Chinese history.

Westad, Odd Arne. Decisive Encounters: The Chinese Civil War, 1946-1950. Stanford: Stanford University Press, 2003. Print.

Pepper, Suzanne. Civil War in China: The Political Struggle, 1945-1949. 2nd ed. Lanham, MD: Rowman & Littlefield Publishers, 1999. Print.

Levine, Steven I. Anvil of Victory: The Communist Revolution in Manchuria, 1945-1948. New York: Columbia University Press, 1987. Print.

Lary, Diana. China's Civil War: A Social History, 1945-1949. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 2015. Print.

Tanner, Harold M. The Battle for Manchuria and the Fate of China: Siping, 1946. Bloomington: Indiana University Press, 2013. Print.

Eastman, Lloyd E. Seeds of Destruction: Nationalist China in War and Revolution, 1937-1949. Stanford: Stanford University Press, 1984. Print.

JOURNAL ARTICLES

Academic Journal Publications

Peer-reviewed articles from leading academic journals.

Shen, Zhihua and Yafeng Xia. "Leadership Transfer in the Asian Revolution: Mao Zedong and the Asian Cominform." Cold War History 14.2 (2014): 195-213. Web.

Sheng, Michael M. "Mao, Stalin, and the Formation of the Anti-Japanese United Front: 1935-37." The China Quarterly 129 (1992): 149-170. Web.

Chassin, Lionel Max. "The Three Great Campaigns of 1948-1949." The Communist Conquest of China: A History of the Civil War 1945-1949. Cambridge: Harvard University Press, 1965. 166-201. Print.

DIGITAL RESOURCES

Online Archives & Databases

Digital collections and online historical resources.

Wilson Center Digital Archive. "Chinese Civil War Collection." History and Public Policy Program Digital Archive. Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars. Web. Accessed 2024.

Stanford University. "Chinese Civil War Resources." Hoover Institution Archives. Web. Accessed 2024.

Library of Congress. "Chinese Civil War Photograph Collection." Prints and Photographs Division. Web. Accessed 2024.
